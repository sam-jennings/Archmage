## The Contest

*The Archmage is gone.*

Without an Archmage to anchor the flow of magic, the world's energy currents are collapsing. Ancient wards falter, magical artifacts dim, and the ambient energy flowing through the universe steadily dissipates into the void. What once renewed itself now dwindles with every passing day.

The Grand Academy understands what must happen: when ambient energy reaches critical depletion, it will seek out and elevate the most powerful living wizard, raising them to Archmage. This isn't a title to be awarded or a position to be judged - it's a magical inevitability. The magic itself will choose, converging upon the wizard whose mastery resonates most powerfully.

To ensure no power is wasted, the Academy has created two interwoven systems: The Array (a stabilized field where five components remain constantly visible), and The Arcane Reserve (a containment matrix that captures drained magical energy). The Source - the naturally occurring ambient energy - steadily depletes as wizards draw from it.

Each wizard arranges controlled components into patterns within their Spellbook - physical proof of mastery. During normal work, these patterns can be activated to gather more components. When the Source empties and infrastructure collapses during the Drought, they stand as evidence of what you've achieved.

When the Released Reserve is finally depleted, the accumulated power in each wizard's Spellbook resonates with the world's concentrated magic. The patterns you've built generate resonance strength. The magic evaluates these resonances and converges upon the strongest.

That wizard ascends. The currents stabilize. Magic flows again.

## Setup

*Wizards gather at the Grand Academy. The Array displays components available for regulated trade. The Source holds the last reserves of ambient energy. Each wizard begins with a handful of components, ready to prove their mastery.*

### Setup Steps

1. Select the appropriate deck for your player count:
   - 2-4 players: Components valued 1-15 (4 energy types x 15 ranks = 60 cards, plus 2 wilds = 62 cards)
   - 5-7 players: Components valued 1-20 (4 energy types x 20 ranks = 80 cards, plus 4 wilds = 84 cards)
2. Deal starting components:
   - All players: 7 cards each
3. Place 5 cards face-up to form The Array
4. Place remaining cards face-down as The Source
5. Randomly determine starting player
6. Each player takes 1 counter (represents your base magical capacity)

## Components

### Understanding what you're working with

#### Magical Components (Cards)

- Each card represents a fragment of magical energy.
- The suit symbol indicates the energy type this component is attuned to:
  - ♠ Spades: Radiance
  - ♥ Hearts: Void
  - ♦ Diamonds: Flux
  - ♣ Clubs: Aether
- The number (1-20) is the component type - different resonance frequencies.
  - Maximum value depends on player count (15 or 20).
  - Wild cards can substitute for any energy type and value combination.

Example: Void 7 is a Void-attuned component with resonance frequency 7.

#### Your Spellbook

The area in front of you where you arrange components into learned spells. This is your proof of mastery - what you've built demonstrates your understanding.

#### Counters

Physical tokens tracking your magical capacity. Used during casting and learning.

#### The Source

The face-down deck of ambient energy reserves. When this empties, the Academy's systems will collapse.

#### The Array

The 5 face-up components available for regulated trade. The Academy maintains this stable channel for safe transformations.

#### Arcane Reserve

The discard pile where reclaimed magical potential accumulates. Whenever spell structure collapses during refinement or transformation, its raw power is captured here. The Reserve exists to concentrate this unstructured potential and will be released once the Source is exhausted.

## Binding to Arcane Reserve

*Before the contest truly begins, the Arcane Reserve must be bound to all participants. Each wizard releases a single component - not as waste, but as a binding act. This creates a sympathetic connection, allowing the Reserve to safely capture drained energy from the wizards' transformative work.*

Before the first player's turn:

**Each player simultaneously chooses 1 component from their hand and places it in the Arcane Reserve (discard pile).**

This binding ensures that when the Source eventually runs dry, sufficient unstructured potential will have accumulated for final preparations before the final evaluation.

*After all players have bound to the Reserve, the first player begins their turn.*

## Spell Types

*Throughout the contest, you'll learn four types of spells. Each represents a different approach to magical mastery. Your Spellbook - the arrangement of components in front of you - demonstrates what you've mastered.*

### 🔮 Conjuration (Same Energy)

*Drawing additional energy from the Source*

- Requirements: 3+ components of the same energy.
- When cast: Draw additional components from the Source.
  - 3-5 components: +1 component.
  - 6+ components: +2 components.
- What it represents: Sustained connection to an energy source, allowing you to channel more of that force.
- Example: Void 2, Void 5, Void 9, Void 13 (4 Void components, not sequential).

### 🔄 Transfiguration (Sequential Values)

*Transforming available energy into different components using the Array*

- Requirements: 3+ components in consecutive sequence (any energies).
  - Examples: 5-6-7 or 12-13-14-15.
  - Cannot loop around (1-20-19 is invalid; 1-2-3 and 18-19-20 are valid).
- When cast: Must exchange components using the Array.
  - 3 components: Discard 2 from hand, take 1 from Array.
  - 4+ components: Discard 1 from hand, take 1 from Array.
- What it represents: Understanding how to transform energy into different components through the Academy's regulated channels.
- Example: Void 5, Flux 6, Radiance 7, Aether 8 (sequential values, different energies).

### ⚡ Enchantment (Matching Values)

*Stabilising energy within yourself, permanently increasing your capacity*

- Requirements: 3-4 components of the same value.
- Always active: Enchantments don't need to be cast - they're woven into your being.
- What they provide:
  - Additional casting capacity: 3 components: cast +1 spell per turn; 4 components: cast all your spells.
  - Additional learning capacity: 3 components: +1 learning action per turn; 4 components: unlimited learning actions.
  - Physical benefit: Gain +1 counter when you learn a 3-component Enchantment.
- What it represents: While casting uses external energy that returns to the world, Enchantment internalises energy permanently - expanding your capacity from within.
- Example: Radiance-7/Void-7/Flux-7 (three 7s from different energies).

### ✨ Perfect Transmutation (Sequential Same Energy)

*The synthesis of pure power and transformation - perfect energy alignment*

- Requirements: 3+ components in sequence, all from the same energy.
  - Example: Aether 8-9-10.
  - Cannot loop around.
- When cast: Receives BOTH Conjuration and Transfiguration benefits (both mandatory).
- What it represents: The pinnacle achievement - perfect alignment within a single energy type.

## Understanding Counters & Magical Capacity

*Your magical capacity represents how much active magic you can channel in a day. It's not a resource that depletes - it's your baseline capability that resets each turn via the Recall step.*

### How Capacity Works

Counters are used TWICE each turn:

1. Casting Phase: Determine how many spells you can activate.
2. **Recall** (transition): Pick up all counters from your cast spells and return them to your pool. This physical act signals the phase change.
3. Learning Phase: Those same counters now determine how many learning actions you can perform.

Important: Counters represent your capacity, not a spent resource — the Recall step returns them to full availability every turn.

### Building Your Capacity

Everyone starts with 1 counter (can cast 1 spell, perform 1 learning action per turn).

You increase capacity by learning Enchantments:

- 3-component Enchantment: +1 counter permanently.
- 4-component Enchantment: Unlimited capacity (take the Unlimited Capacity marker and place it in front of you; set all your counters aside).

You can also lose capacity:

- Unlearn/break apart 3-component Enchantment: -1 counter.
- Unlearn/break apart 4-component Enchantment: Return the Unlimited Capacity marker and retrieve your counters.

#### Example progression:

- Start: 1 counter.
- After first 3-card Enchantment: 2 counters.
- After second 3-card Enchantment: 3 counters.
- After 4-card Enchantment: Unlimited capacity (Unlimited Capacity marker placed in front of you; counters set aside).
- If you later unlearn that 4-card Enchantment: Return the marker; retrieve your 3 counters.

## Your Turn in Detail

### 1. Collection Phase

*Each morning, you draw upon the Source's dwindling reserves or make careful selections from the Academy's regulated Array.*

Choose one:

- Draw 1 component from The Source, OR
- Take 1 component from The Array (immediately replace from The Source).

That's all for this phase - you'll draw more if you cast Conjurations in the next phase.

### 2. Casting Phase

*Now you activate the spells in your Spellbook. Each spell you cast takes effect immediately, before you cast the next one. The order matters.*

How many spells you can cast:

- 1 counter = up to 1 spell.
- 2 counters = up to 2 spells.
- 3 counters = up to 3 spells.
- Unlimited capacity = all your spells.

To cast spells:

1. Choose one spell from your Spellbook.
2. Place 1 counter on it (marks it as cast this turn).
3. Immediately resolve its effects (see below).
4. Repeat if you have more counters or unlimited capacity.
5. Continue until you've cast all desired spells.

Casting restrictions:

- Each spell can be cast once per turn maximum (one counter per spell).
- Enchantments are always active - don't cast them, they just work.
- If you can't legally resolve a spell's effects, you cannot cast it.

#### Spell Effects (Immediate When Cast)

##### 🔮 Conjuration

Immediately draw bonus components from The Source:

- 3-5 cards: +1 component.
- 6+ cards: +2 components.

##### 🔄 Transfiguration

Must exchange with The Array:

- 3 cards: Discard 2 from hand → take 1 from Array.
- 4+ cards: Discard 1 from hand → take 1 from Array.

Discarded components go to Arcane Reserve (not back to Array).

Replace the Array card from The Source.

If you can't discard enough components, you cannot cast this spell.

##### ✨ Perfect Transmutation

Resolve both effects in order:

1. Conjuration effect: Draw bonus from Source (+1 for 3-5 cards, +2 for 6+ cards).
2. Transfiguration effect: Must exchange with Array (same rules as Transfiguration).

Both effects are mandatory.

If you can't complete the exchange, you cannot cast this spell.

### 3. Learning Phase

*With the day's active magic complete, you turn to formalizing new knowledge or refining existing spells.*

**Recall (transition step):** Pick up all counters from your cast spells and return them to your pool. If you have unlimited capacity, skip this — your Unlimited Capacity marker is already in front of you and your counters remain set aside.

Your counters now determine learning actions:

- 1 counter = up to 1 learning action.
- 2 counters = up to 2 learning actions.
- 3 counters = up to 3 learning actions.
- Unlimited capacity = unlimited learning actions.

Each learning action costs 1 counter (or is free with unlimited capacity):

#### LEARN

Place a new spell from your hand into your Spellbook.

- Must be a valid spell type (Conjuration, Transfiguration, Enchantment, or Perfect Transmutation).
- If 3-component Enchantment: Immediately gain +1 counter (add to your pool now).
- If 4-component Enchantment: Take the Unlimited Capacity marker and place it in front of you; set all counters aside.

#### EMPOWER

Add components from your hand to an existing spell in your Spellbook.

- Can make a Conjuration longer, extend a Transfiguration, or convert one spell type to another.

#### RESHAPE

Reorganize components across multiple spells in your Spellbook.

- Break down any/all spells and rebuild them using the same components.
- All components must remain in valid spell arrangements.
- Counts as one learning action regardless of how many spells affected.
- Warning: If you break apart a 3-component Enchantment, immediately lose 1 counter.
- Warning: If you break apart a 4-component Enchantment, immediately return the Unlimited Capacity marker and retrieve your counters.

#### UNLEARN

Dissolve one spell completely, returning its components to your hand.

- Components can't be learned into new spells until your next turn.
- Useful for reclaiming components for a different strategy.
- Each Unlearn action targets one spell and costs 1 counter. Unlearning two different spells in the same turn requires 2 separate Unlearn actions at 1 counter each.
- Warning: If you unlearn a 3-component Enchantment, immediately lose 1 counter.
- Warning: If you unlearn a 4-component Enchantment, immediately return the Unlimited Capacity marker and retrieve your counters.

#### 💡 The Recall Step

The Recall is the physical bridge between Casting and Learning. Pick up every counter sitting on a cast spell and return it to your pool — that's the whole step.

- Counters represent your capacity, not a depleting resource.
- Casting fewer spells doesn't give you more learning actions — you always get your full capacity back.
- Unlimited capacity players skip the Recall entirely; the Unlimited Capacity marker already signals their state.

## The Drought & Released Reserve

*When the Source empties, ambient energy is no longer accessible. The Academy's stabilizing systems fail, and the regulated Array - never a source of magic itself, only a conduit - can no longer be sustained. What remains is the Arcane Reserve: reclaimed, unstructured potential released to fuel the final preparations before judgment.*

### The Drought

Trigger: The instant The Source runs dry.

What happens:

1. Shuffle The Array together with the Arcane Reserve (all discarded components).
2. This becomes the Released Reserve - a limited pool of unstable magical potential.
3. The Array no longer exists as a separate entity.
4. Turn order continues as normal.

*In this final phase, magic behaves differently. Grand manipulations of the world are no longer possible. Only what is bound within a wizard - through internalized mastery and enduring enchantment - continues to function reliably.*

#### Modified turns during The Drought

1. Collection Phase:
   - Draw exactly 1 component from the Released Reserve.
   - No choice between Source and Array (both are gone).
2. Learning Phase:
   - Works completely normally.
   - You can still Learn, Empower, Reshape, or Unlearn.
   - This is your last chance to optimize your Spellbook for final evaluation.
   - Your Enchantments still provide their normal learning capacity benefits.

#### What happened to Casting?

With ambient flow exhausted and all regulated channels collapsed, structured spellcasting can no longer be sustained during the Drought.

- Conjurations drew from the Source's organised energy reserves - now scattered and chaotic.
- Transfigurations required the Array's stable framework - now dissolved.
- Enchantments are always active automatically - they don't need casting.
- Perfect Transmutations need both Source and Array - both are gone.

With no spells able to be cast, this phase simply doesn't exist during the Drought. You collect 1 component, then proceed directly to learning.

#### End of The Drought

When the Released Reserve is depleted (all cards drawn), the contest immediately ends. Proceed to the final evaluation.

## Winning the Game

*The Academy must choose. The moment has arrived to determine who will ascend.*

### Standard Evaluation

The Academy evaluates whose Spellbook demonstrates the greatest breadth and depth of understanding. Each spell type has a value based on its size and complexity.

## Glossary

**The Source:** The face-down deck containing the last reserves of ambient energy. When it empties, the Academy's systems collapse.

**The Array:** The 5 face-up components. The Academy's regulated channel for safe transformations. Collapses when The Source empties.

**Arcane Reserve:** The discard pile. Contains unstructured magical potential from spent components. Released when The Source empties.

**The Drought:** The final phase when The Source empties. The Array and Arcane Reserve merge into the Released Reserve. No casting occurs - only collection and learning.

**Released Reserve:** The final draw pile (Array + Arcane Reserve shuffled together). Limited, unstable magic for final preparations.

**Conjuration:** Spell using 3+ components of the same energy (not sequential). When cast, draws bonus components from The Source.

**Transfiguration:** Spell using 3+ sequential components of different energies. When cast, must exchange components using The Array.

**Enchantment:** Spell using 3-4 matching components. Always active (never cast). Permanently increases capacity.

**Perfect Transmutation:** Spell using 3+ sequential components of the same energy. When cast, provides both Conjuration AND Transfiguration effects.

**Casting:** Activating spells from your Spellbook by placing counters on them one at a time. Effects resolve immediately.

**Spellbook:** Your tableau of learned spells - the proof of your mastery.

**Counters:** Tokens representing your magical capacity. Used for both casting and learning. Gained by learning Enchantments, lost by breaking them apart.

**Magical Capacity:** How many spells you can cast and how many learning actions you can perform each turn. Starts at 1, increases with Enchantments.

**Unlimited Capacity:** State granted by 4-component Enchantments. Cast all spells, perform unlimited learning actions. Can be lost if that Enchantment is broken apart.

**Recognition Points:** Points awarded for spell mastery in the final evaluation.

## Quick Reference

### Turn Summary (Normal Play)

1. Collection: Draw 1 from Source OR take 1 from Array.
2. Casting: Cast spells one at a time, resolve effects immediately.
3. **Recall:** Pick up all counters from cast spells and return them to your pool.
4. Learning: Use counters for Learn/Empower/Reshape/Unlearn actions (1 counter each).

### Turn Summary (The Drought)

1. Collection: Draw 1 from Released Reserve.
2. Learning: Use counters for Learn/Empower/Reshape/Unlearn actions.
3. (No Casting Phase - spells can't function during The Drought.)

### Spell Effects (Normal Play)

- Conjuration: +1 component (3-5 cards) or +2 components (6+ cards) from Source.
- Transfiguration: Exchange with Array (discard 2 → take 1 for 3 cards; discard 1 → take 1 for 4+ cards).
- Enchantment: Always active, increases capacity.
- Perfect Transmutation: Both Conjuration + Transfiguration effects (both mandatory).

### Counter Tracking

- Start: 1 counter.
- +1 per 3-component Enchantment learned.
- 4-component Enchantment: take the Unlimited Capacity marker; set all counters aside.
- -1 if 3-component Enchantment broken apart.
- 4-component Enchantment broken apart: return the Unlimited Capacity marker; retrieve your counters.
