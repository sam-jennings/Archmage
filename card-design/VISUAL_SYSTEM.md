# Archmage Ascension — Visual System Specification

## Purpose of this document

This document defines the visual system for **Archmage Ascension** after choosing the **Radiance / Void / Flux / Aether** model rather than a classical elemental model.

It is intended to guide:

- card art direction
- symbol design
- palette choices
- UI / prototype visuals
- moodboards and AI prompts
- rulebook visual language
- future story terminology updates

The aim is not just to make the game look attractive. The aim is to make the game’s visuals **express the same mental model as the mechanics**.

The game is about **attunement, resonance, magical structure, transformation, and convergence**. The visual system should therefore present magic as a **formal arcane system** rather than as naturalistic elemental fantasy or technological sci-fi.

---

## Core design decision

The four suits are **not classical elements** such as earth, fire, water, and air.

They are also not four factions with different personalities or asymmetric mechanical identities.

They are best understood as **four currents of magic**: four equally fundamental ways in which ambient magic can become organized, visible, and usable.

This is important because the game’s mechanics are based on:

- grouping by same current
- grouping by same resonance frequency
- grouping by sequential resonance frequencies
- combining and transforming patterns

The visuals should therefore support a player mental model of:

- **pattern recognition**
- **attunement**
- **magical behavior**
- **formal relationships**

rather than a mental model of:

- natural substances
- terrain types
- temperament-based factions
- “fire beats water” style assumptions

### Why this is stronger than an elemental model

A classical elemental model creates useful immediate familiarity, but it also creates false expectations.

Players naturally assume that fire, water, earth, and air have different personalities, strengths, weaknesses, or thematic roles. That is useful in many games, but it is slightly misleading here because the suits in Archmage Ascension are **mechanically equal kinds, not mechanically different powers**.

The current model avoids that problem. It allows the suits to feel distinct without implying that one is more aggressive, defensive, emotional, stable, or inherently stronger than another.

That is a better fit for a game built around resonance patterns and structured magical mastery.

## What type of magic this is

### Short description

The game uses a style of magic best described as:

**resonance-based ritual magic**

### Expanded description

This is a world where magic is understood as an ambient arcane force that flows through reality and can be:

- perceived
- crystallized
- attuned to
- structured
- transformed
- internalized

Magic is not primarily depicted as throwing fireballs or commanding the weather.

Instead, it is depicted as a **formal metaphysical system** with:

- currents
- resonance frequencies
- stable and unstable states
- ritual structures
- geometric organization
- visible proofs of mastery

### Useful internal descriptors

Use language like:

- **ritual arcana**
- **resonance magic**
- **formal magic**
- **arcane metaphysics**
- **structured magic**

### Useful image-search / prompt descriptors

If building moodboards or AI prompts, the most useful terms are:

- **arcane sigil fantasy**
- **ritual geometry fantasy**
- **cosmic occult fantasy**
- **alchemical magic circles**
- **dark fantasy sigils**
- **mystical astronomical diagrams**
- **engraved magical symbols**

These terms tend to produce imagery that feels:

- mystical
- diagrammatic
- arcane
- ceremonial
- cosmic

without drifting into classical elemental fantasy or sci-fi UI design.

## The four currents as a system

The four currents should be understood as four related modes of magical behavior:

- **Radiance** — magic that emits
- **Void** — magic that absorbs
- **Flux** — magic that moves
- **Aether** — magic that binds and stabilizes

Together, these four currents form a coherent magical ecology:

- magic may push outward
- draw inward
- travel between states
- or hold a form

This makes the four currents feel like parts of one system rather than four unrelated themes.

---

## Collective terminology

The preferred collective term is:

## **Currents**

Example usage:

- “Each component belongs to one of the four currents.”
- “A Conjuration sustains attunement to one current.”
- “The wild card represents convergence of the four currents.”

### Why “currents” works well

- It implies distinct kinds rather than higher/lower power levels.
- It fits the story language of arcane currents flowing through the world.
- It suggests motion and magical behavior rather than material substance.
- It avoids implying that higher values are simply “more energy.”

### Story terminology note

Because **Aether** is retained as one of the four current names, the broader story term **“the aether”** should be renamed to avoid overlap.

Recommended replacements:

- **the ether**
- **the arcane field**
- **the firmament**

Preferred option for minimal disruption:

## **the ether**

That keeps Aether as a clean current name while avoiding terminological collision.

# The Four Currents

Each current should be defined in the same order:

1. **Name**
2. **Colour**
3. **Symbol**
4. **Visual behavior / pattern**
5. **Meaning**
6. **Reason it works in the system**

---

## 1. Radiance

### Name
**Radiance**

### Colour
**Gold / amber / warm luminous yellow**

This should not be flat bright yellow. It should feel like:

- sunlit metal
- torchlight
- gilded glow
- warm magical bloom

### Symbol
**Radial burst / corona glyph**

Recommended symbol language:

- central point with outward rays
- concentric radiant circles
- halo burst
- stylized sun-like sigil without becoming “sun worship” iconography

### Visual behavior / pattern
Radiance should always read as **outward emission**.

Its design language should include:

- concentric rings
- radial lines
- halos
- corona-like glows
- cardinal bursts
- warm bloom that pushes away from a centre

### Meaning
Radiance is the **emissive state of magic**.

It extends, illuminates, reveals, and projects.

### Why it works
Radiance is visually immediate and easy to understand without collapsing into literal fire.

It gives the system a current associated with expansion and outward projection, which pairs naturally against Void’s inward pull.

### What to avoid
Do not let Radiance become:

- literal flames
- orange-red infernos
- aggressive explosion imagery
- solar deity symbolism

It should feel like **ritual light**, not fire magic.

---

## 2. Void

### Name
**Void**

### Colour
**Deep violet / indigo / cold purple-black**

This should feel like:

- depth
- absence
- celestial darkness
- amethyst shadow

### Symbol
**Spiral vortex / hollow sigil**

Recommended symbol language:

- inward spiral
- circular hollow centre
- vortex mark
- aperture or eclipse-like form

### Visual behavior / pattern
Void should always read as **inward absorption**.

Its design language should include:

- spirals
- hollow centres
- depth gradients
- star scatter
- negative space
- orbit-like curves that appear to draw inward

### Meaning
Void is the **absorptive state of magic**.

It gathers, quiets, draws inward, and creates depth.

### Why it works
Void gives the system a current associated with recession, stillness, and gathering, without automatically meaning evil or corruption.

It balances Radiance elegantly.

### What to avoid
Do not let Void become:

- necromantic darkness
- demonic shadow imagery
- skulls, corruption, or decay
- muddy black-purple mush

It should feel like **mystical depth**, not villain energy.

---

## 3. Flux

### Name
**Flux**

### Colour
**Teal / turquoise / blue-green luminescence**

This should feel like:

- moving current
- cold living motion
- liquid light without literal water
- oscillating magical flow

### Symbol
**Wave glyph / oscillation mark**

Recommended symbol language:

- sine-wave-inspired line
- ripple motif
- flowing strand
- layered wave or pulse mark

### Visual behavior / pattern
Flux should always read as **transition and movement**.

Its design language should include:

- waves
- ripples
- oscillating bands
- directional flowing lines
- repeated rhythmic curves
- pulse-like motion

### Meaning
Flux is the **transitional state of magic**.

It moves, transfers, shifts, and carries power between forms and frequencies.

### Why it works
Flux is the current that most naturally expresses:

- transformation
- transition
- sequence
- moving from one state to another

That makes it a strong fit for a game whose mechanics care about ordered values and changing configurations.

### What to avoid
Do not let Flux become:

- literal ocean / sea / foam imagery
- soft “nature healing” fantasy
- sci-fi plasma streams
- electric blue tech beams

It should feel like **arcane flow**, not water magic or technology.

---

## 4. Aether

### Name
**Aether**

### Colour
**Crimson / deep red / blood-warm arcane red**

This should feel like:

- structured energy under tension
- glowing seal-wax red
- engraved arcane geometry
- disciplined magical force

### Symbol
**Lattice star / node pattern / geometric binding sigil**

Recommended symbol language:

- node-connected star
- geometric framework
- crystalline or networked sigil
- interlocking lines with a held centre

### Visual behavior / pattern
Aether should always read as **binding and stabilization**.

Its design language should include:

- lattices
- nodes
- connecting lines
- geometric scaffolds
- crystalline structures
- contained radiating geometry

### Meaning
Aether is the **structured state of magic**.

It binds, stabilizes, contains, and gives form.

### Why it works
Aether gives the system a current that feels like magical architecture.

Where Flux moves and Void gathers, Aether holds. It is the current of containment, formal structure, and durable magical organization.

### What to avoid
Do not let Aether become:

- generic “red fire”
- military or aggressive iconography
- electrical circuitry
- techno-grid UI design

It should feel like **arcane structure**, not electricity or battle magic.

# The wild card

## Core concept

The wild card is **not** a fifth current.

It represents **Convergence**: a temporary or exceptional alignment of all four currents.

That distinction is critical.

If the wild card is treated like a separate suit with its own visual language, it weakens the clarity of the whole system. The deck should always read as having **four** currents plus a rare state in which all four coincide.

## Wild card meaning

The wild card should communicate:

- synthesis
- total alignment
- magical convergence
- unstable perfection
- ceremonial rarity

## Wild card palette

The base palette should be:

**cool silver / moonstone / pale arcane neutral**

This is preferable to giving it a fifth saturated colour.

Why:

- it marks the wild as distinct from the four currents
- it avoids competing with the established current palettes
- it makes the four-colour accents feel special rather than noisy

## Wild card visual language

The wild card should combine visible motifs from all four currents:

- Radiance rings / rays
- Void spirals / depth / stars
- Flux waves / oscillations
- Aether lattice / nodes / geometry

These should be integrated into a **balanced, ceremonial composition**, not smashed together chaotically.

## Wild card compositional rule

Each ordinary current card should have **one dominant behavior**.

The wild card should have **multiple balanced behaviors**.

This makes it read immediately as:

- rarer
- more symmetrical
- more complete
- more ceremonial

## What to avoid for the wild card

Do not make the wild card:

- rainbow-heavy
- louder than every normal card
- a fifth icon system
- visually chaotic

It should feel like **disciplined convergence**, not visual overload.

The player should feel like they are handling:

not:

# Card visual design guidance

## 1. Card fronts should be system-first, illustration-second

The card front should not behave like a trading card with a unique scene illustration.

Instead, it should behave like a **magical instrument panel designed by ritual scholars**.

That means:

- strong shared architecture
- suit-specific behavior motifs
- subtle ornamental structure
- very little narrative illustration

### Why
The game is about recognizing patterns and relationships quickly. The visuals should support that by being coherent and legible.

---

## 2. Use one shared card architecture across the whole deck

All currents should share the same overall card layout:

- same frame shape
- same medallion structure
- same connector placement
- same value positions
- same typography structure
- same border logic

### Why
This makes the deck feel like one designed magical system instead of four unrelated mini-worlds.

Consistency also supports readability during play.

---

## 3. Use suit-specific motifs inside a common structure

Within that shared architecture, each current gets a different **behavior language**, not a different whole card layout.

That means:

- same card skeleton
- different internal motif family

### Why
This is the cleanest way to say “different currents of the same magical reality.”

---

## 4. Geometry should dominate, but not feel sterile

The design should be **mostly geometric**, with some controlled soft magical behavior.

Use:

- circles
- arcs
- lattices
- wave paths
- nodes
- measured ornaments
- alignment marks

Then soften them with:

- bloom
- glow
- smoke-light gradients
- subtle depth
- imperfect magical spill

### Why
If the cards are too geometric, they risk feeling like magical software.
If they are too painterly, they drift into generic fantasy art.

The target is a balance:

**formal arcane structure + living energy behavior**

---

## 5. Additional front imagery should be diagrammatic, not scenic

It is good to add more visual information to the front of the card, but it should be:

- background sigils
- calibration marks
- ghost circles
- orbit lines
- etched symbolic geometry
- subtle occult ornament

It should **not** be:

- characters
- landscapes
- flames / waves / rocks / clouds as literal scenes
- action illustrations

### Why
The current model is strongest when it feels like the player is manipulating magical patterns, not collecting fantasy paintings.

---

## 6. Background colour should stay unified across the deck

The deck should use a **shared dark base family** rather than radically different background worlds per current.

Recommended approach:

- all cards use a very dark background base
- each current slightly tints the background shadows and inner glow
- major colour identity comes from the motif, connectors, borders, and symbols

### Why
This preserves deck unity while still making each current easy to distinguish.

If each current has a fully different background treatment, the system starts feeling fragmented.

---

## 7. Different values in the same current should share the same design family

Cards within a current should not each have a unique illustration.

Instead, they should feel like:

**the same current observed at different resonance frequencies**

### Good ways to vary value

Value can affect controlled properties such as:

- alignment position
- density of secondary marks
- number of rings / nodes / wave intervals
- subtle complexity level
- connector position
- emphasis of internal structure

### Why
This preserves suit identity while making values feel systematic.

It also reinforces the game fiction that the number represents resonance frequency, not rank personality.

---

## 8. Connector design should feel like current alignment, not arbitrary decoration

Because the connectors express matching and sequencing when cards overlap, they should remain an important part of the system.

The connector strip should feel like:

- exposed magical alignment
- visible resonance
- current meeting current

### Why
This is one of the strongest places where the visual design can reinforce the mechanics directly.

When sequential or same-value cards align, the resulting visual bloom should feel like a real magical event rather than a UI highlight.

---

## 9. Typography should feel ceremonial and legible

Typography should communicate:

- authority
- ritual formality
- magical scholarship

It should not feel:

- futuristic
- techno-minimal
- playful
- rustic

Recommended direction:

- elegant serif or engraved display face for numbers and headings
- restrained secondary text
- spacing and alignment that suggest formality

### Why
The cards should feel like artifacts of a magical discipline, not cards from a sci-fi card battler.

---

## 10. Borders and ornamental lines should feel etched rather than metallic-tech

Use borders that suggest:

- engraved frames
- inlaid magical architecture
- ritual seal-work

Avoid borders that suggest:

- chrome
- bevelled plastic
- glowing circuitry
- holographic panels

### Why
The border is one of the easiest places for the design to accidentally become sci-fi.

---

# Specific design choices that keep it from feeling sci-fi

This is one of the most important parts of the whole system.

The theme can drift into sci-fi very easily if the execution goes in the wrong direction.

## Hard rules to avoid sci-fi drift

### 1. No hologram logic
Avoid visuals that look like:

- projected interfaces
- floating UI panels
- scanning overlays
- translucent tech screens

### 2. No chrome or industrial materials
Avoid:

- metallic silver frames that look machined
- gunmetal surfaces
- plastic gloss
- brushed steel looks

### 3. No electric neon for its own sake
Glow should feel magical and atmospheric, not like LED lighting.

Use:

- bloom
- aura
- candlelit softness
- occult luminosity

Avoid:

- hard RGB gradients
- cyberpunk saturation
- laser-edge glow

### 4. No hard techno iconography
Avoid symbols that resemble:

- circuit boards
- scientific UI symbols
- digital graphs
- radar screens
- chip patterns

Aether’s lattice must feel **arcane and sigilic**, not technological.

### 5. Use texture and age subtly
Even in a dark system, the art should imply:

- engraved surfaces
- vellum memory
- worn ritual materials
- smoky depth
- magical residue

This does not mean making the game parchment-based. It means giving it tactile fantasy presence rather than sterile digital smoothness.

### 6. Prefer occult geometry over machine precision
Shapes should be ordered and intentional, but not perfectly clinical.

Think:

- ritual circles
- astronomical diagrams
- alchemical plates
- engraved sigils

not:

- CAD drawings
- HUD displays
- perfect machine interface grids

---

# Practical visual summary

If the visual design is working correctly, the player should be able to feel the following instantly:

- the deck belongs to one magical system
- each current has its own distinct behavior
- the cards are about patterns and attunement, not scenes or factions
- the wild card is a convergence of the four currents
- the whole game feels arcane and metaphysical, not elemental or technological

---

# Final art-direction statement

**Archmage Ascension should look like a disciplined dark-fantasy system of arcane currents: engraved ritual diagrams animated by living magical behavior. Its four currents — Radiance, Void, Flux, and Aether — should feel distinct, equally fundamental, and visibly related. The cards should prioritize formal structure, luminous motif language, and resonance-based variation over literal illustration. The wild card should represent convergence rather than a fifth current. Above all, the system must feel ceremonial, mystical, and metaphysical — never generic elemental fantasy, and never sci-fi.**
