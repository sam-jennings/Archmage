# Ascension Trials

The Ascension Trials are the advanced endgame variant for experienced players. Instead of using only the standard final evaluation, players calculate power across domains and secretly allocate that power to trials.

> [!info] Core files
> - Main game rules: [[Archmage Ascension - Complete Rulebook]]
> - Standard scoring table: [[Scoring System Reference]]

---

## Overview

Your final Spellbook generates magical power across different domains. You secretly allocate this power to trials. The wizard with the highest legal power on each trial wins its Recognition Points. Most Recognition Points wins.

### Strategy Primer: Building Toward the Trials

From the first turn, your Spellbook is also quietly shaping your options in the Ascension Trial. Each spell you learn can strengthen a particular kind of power: elemental domains through Conjurations, transformation through Transfigurations, enchantment through Enchantments, and flexible pressure through Perfect Transmutations and some larger Enchantments.

A broad Spellbook gives you more ways to enter different trials and respond to what others might be building. A concentrated Spellbook can make one or two domains much harder to beat, but may leave you with fewer legal or efficient places to apply that strength.

Neither path is automatically better. As you build, keep asking: am I creating many possible trial entries, or am I making a smaller number of entries more commanding? The answer can change as the table reveals its ambitions.

## Power Formula

| Spell size | Power multiplier | Examples |
|:----------:|:----------------:|---------|
| 3-4 components | x 1.0 (= spell length) | 3 cards = 3 power, 4 cards = 4 power |
| 5-6 components | x 1.5 (round normally) | 5 cards = 7.5 → 8 power, 6 cards = 9 power |
| 7+ components  | x 2.0 | 7 cards = 14 power |

## Power Domains

| Domain | Source |
|--------|--------|
| Void Power | Void Conjuration spell lengths (using formula above) |
| Flux Power | Flux Conjuration spell lengths |
| Radiance Power | Radiance Conjuration spell lengths |
| Aether Power | Aether Conjuration spell lengths |
| Transformation Power | All Transfiguration spell lengths |
| Enchantment Power | All Enchantment spell sizes |

### Perfect Transmutation Bonuses

- Counts as a Conjuration for its energy's domain (+formula power).
- Counts as a Transfiguration for Transformation domain (+formula power).
- Grants +1 wild magic power.

### 4-Component Enchantment Bonus

- Grants +1 wild magic power.

## The Six Trials

| Trial | Power Source Requirement | Proves |
|-------|--------------------------|--------|
| Energy Purity | Exactly ONE energy domain | Single-energy mastery |
| Energy Harmony | 2+ different energy domains | Multi-energy balance |
| Transformation | Transformation domain only | Transmutation mastery |
| Enchantment | Enchantment domain only | Internal enhancement mastery |
| Focused Power | Exactly ONE domain (any type) | True specialization |
| Universal Power | 2+ different domains (any mix) | Versatility |

### 7th Trial (5-7 Players Only)

| Trial | Power Source Requirement | Proves |
|-------|--------------------------|--------|
| Complete Mastery | ALL three domain types (1+ energy + Transformation + Enchantment) | Comprehensive mastery |

## Wild Magic Rules

- Earned by: each Perfect Transmutation (+1), each 4-component Enchantment (+1).
- Can only be added to trials you're **already attempting** (must have 1+ normal power).
- Does NOT count as an extra source (won't make Harmony/Universal legal alone).
- Does NOT violate mono requirements (safe to add to Purity or Focused).
- Can be split across multiple trials or stacked on one.
- Ignored in tiebreaker calculations.

## Tiebreaker Order

1. **Utilization** - power allocated from domains used ÷ total capacity of those domains (higher wins).
2. **Focus** - power allocated to this trial ÷ total power across all domains (higher wins).
3. **Breadth** - Harmony/Universal only; more distinct sources wins.
4. **Shared Recognition** - both players receive full Recognition Points.

## Balance Notes

- Perfect Transmutations of 9-10 components were identified as overpowered and rebalanced.
- Easy Conjuration extensions also rebalanced in v2.x iterations.
- Any changes to the scoring table require rebalancing analysis across all player counts.
- The Ascension Trial Recognition Points pool is calibrated per player count to maintain strategic tension.
