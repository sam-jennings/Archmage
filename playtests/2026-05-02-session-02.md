# Session 2 — 2026-05-02 (planned)

## Goal
Does the counter system become clearer with more players watching each other use it — and does 5-player count change the Drought/enchantment dynamics that collapsed Phase 2 in Session 1?

## Hypothesis being tested
If the counter refresh rule is rewritten with a concrete turn-step example, new players will understand it without verbal explanation.

## Variable being tested
Player count (2 → 5). The counter rule has NOT been rewritten — this session is observational: watch *whether* confusion occurs and *at what point*, to inform the rewrite.

## Test stage (course §12)
Local Iteration Testing

## Pre-session checklist
- [ ] Teach enchantment timing explicitly: "Effects from playing or unlearning an enchantment start on your NEXT turn — not this one"
- [ ] When teaching counters, do a physical demo: draw, cast, recall, learn — narrate every step
- [ ] Note the score table doesn't go above PT size ~14 — if someone scores high, calculate manually
- [ ] Have something to write on during play — you're observing, not just playing

## What to watch for

### Confusion signals
- Any player treats counters as a resource to save rather than a capacity that resets
- Any player re-asks the counter refresh rule after it's been explained
- Any player abuses enchantment timing (plays/unlearns an enchantment and immediately tries to use the effect this turn)
- Players forgetting which stage they're on (Cast / Recall / Learn)

### Engagement signals
- Players reacting to what others build ("ooh" at the Array or a big Spellbook)
- Players planning ahead across turns — talking about what they'll do next
- More interaction/blocking with 5 players than was possible at 2

### Pacing signals
- Phase 2 (post-Drought): does it feel like active spellbook organisation, or does it stall again?
- How many players have 4-card enchantments at Drought? (This caused Phase 2 to stall at 2 players)
- Total session length — expect longer at 5 players; note where time is lost

## What success looks like
Players use counters correctly after the initial explanation, without re-asking. At least some players engage with spellbook organisation during Phase 2 rather than waiting to act on the final turn. The session produces data on whether 5-player count changes the enchantment-at-Drought problem.

## What failure looks like
Counter confusion persists across multiple players despite the verbal demo. Phase 2 stalls again with 5 players — suggesting the enchantment cap issue is not a 2-player artefact but a structural problem.

## What NOT to look at this session
- Reference card readability (known issue, fix pending — don't let it distract the session focus)
- Thematic narrative (deferred — not what this session is for)
- Balance / who won (Stage 3 — irrelevant right now)

## Mid-game check-in question
"What are you trying to do right now?" — tests whether players have a strategy or are just reacting. Ask around the midpoint, once the Spellbook is starting to grow.

## Post-game debrief
1. What was your favourite part?
2. What was your least favourite part?
3. What confused you?
4. Was it fun? Why or why not?
5. "When you refreshed your counters — did that ever feel surprising, or did it make sense straight away?"
